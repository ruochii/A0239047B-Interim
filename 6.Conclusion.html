5. Conclusion

In conclusion, we have successfully fabricated MeHA with various degrees of Me and LAP substitution, and obtained precise pore sizes by integrating in-house developed templating method. In the upcoming semester, we will focus on further optimizing the stiffness of these gels, considering how pore size may influence it. At the same time, we will determine the optimal PQ concentration to control gel degradation. Considering how these three parameters interact, we will optimize all parameters—stiffness, porosity, and degradation rate—such that independent control of these parameters is achieved. Following this optimization, our research will explore how the parameters impact tumor explant viability, morphology, composition, nutrient diffusion and gene expression in ex vivo culture. We aim to conduct tissue culture experiments with three types of tissues, tailoring the matrix properties to optimize preservation. This approach will enhance our understanding of the influence of matrix characteristics on tumor explant behavior, providing insights that could lead to more refined hydrogel-based models for advancing therapeutic strategies.
