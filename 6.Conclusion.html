6. Conclusion and Recommendation

This research successfully developed the MeHAir system, a tunable MeHA hydrogel platform engineered to preserve the TME of diverse PDTE. By adjusting the degree of methacrylation and LAP concentration, the hydrogel stiffness could be matched to tissue needs. The optimized GMP fabrication process enabled defined porosity and crosslinking, which are essential for adapting to tumor-specific TME for cell migration and nutrient diffusion. Preliminary melanoma explant cultures proved the ability of MeHA hydrogels to maintain the surface area of the slices and promote cell growth over 12 days. Future studies should explore pore size effects on stiffness and viability, RGD concentration tuning, and long-term explant functionality. These refinements will enhance the hydrogel’s reproducibility and establish its translational relevance for mechanistic cancer research and preclinical drug testing.
